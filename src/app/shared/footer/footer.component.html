The FAQ

Tell me this first, what is the current Angular version?

Angular stable version is currently 2.2.1 and the latest version is 2.3.0-beta.0.

Now tell me, what on earth is this about?

This site shows off a list of modules that you can use in your Angular 2 projects and some details related to it. The modules are categorized according to their purpose. Also, distinct colours are used to depict the status of each module.

And that freakish number beside each module name?

It is the most recent stable version that module supports. It may not be accurate for a few modules that haven’t clearly specified the version it supports. However, don’t despair. Most of it should be correct and we got the supported versions from an awesome thread in the Angular 2 subreddit.

Then what’s that annoying image and quote about?

Wait, we thought you know about Star Wars already. And who doesn’t like Star Wars?

Who are the culprits behind this?

We were a hackathon team in Angular Attack 2016. We called ourselves as Exilee Coders. It took a long time to get this released, but we wanted to somehow do it. And here it is now.

How can I rant or help you guys?

We totally understand in case you’d want to rant at us. Also, we’d like to get your feedback. Maybe even some help to make this site better for Angular devs. The site’s repo on github is immysl/ng2use. Please feel free to take a peek.

Is this site stable or broken?

We think it is stable enough for use and it should work even on a small mobile screen. The site layout is responsive but it may have a few minor glitches. We will keep working on the fixes and hopefully will be able to add a few more things in the future.

Do you guys charge to share this site?

No not at all. In fact, we love you for thinking of sharing this site. So please feel free to share the love.

Is there any last words before I finish reading this boring “The FAQ”?

Yes, we do have a few. We are fans of Shai Reznik’s talks on ng-conf. Do check out his talk called ng-wat (ng-conf 2015) and of course The ng-show (ng-conf 2016). Oh, and don’t forget to watch those amazing Star Wars movies. There’s one coming out soon this year.
